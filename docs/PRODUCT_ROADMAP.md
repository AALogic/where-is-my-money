# Product Roadmap

## Roadmap Philosophy

The roadmap grows from real workflows, observed friction, and repeated sales questions.

This is not a feature wish list. Each roadmap item needs to improve one of three outcomes:

1. Faster account understanding.
2. Better sales decisions.
3. More trustworthy reports.

Features that only make the product look richer can wait.

## Current Product Slice

The current slice is intentionally narrow:

- buyer NIP lookup,
- buyer procurement history,
- top winning suppliers,
- cash-flow relationship graph,
- regional and segment expansion,
- AI enrichment from current board context,
- PDF report generation.

The product starts here because buyer-centric preparation is the clearest sales workflow.

## Near-Term Roadmap

The next step is not to expand everywhere. The next step is to make the buyer workflow sharper.

### Buyer Intelligence

Improve buyer profiles, spend timelines, distinction between notices/procedures/results/wins, and category breakdowns.

Product bet: better buyer profiles increase completed account intelligence sessions.

### Supplier Intelligence

Show where a supplier wins, which buyers they depend on, what categories they dominate, and whether they look like a competitor or possible partner.

Product bet: supplier context helps users move from "who won" to "what sales move is possible".

### Network Exploration

Add graph layers for cash flow, categories, regions, and segments. Improve drill-down from graph node to tender detail. Let users save analysis boards and pass graph context into AI prompts.

Product bet: graph layers reveal relationship patterns faster than table-only analysis.

### AI Research

Add prompt registry, prompt versioning, creator-defined research tasks, evidence-aware findings, and session-level research memory.

Product bet: AI becomes more useful when it receives graph context instead of a blank prompt.

### Reporting

Add configurable report templates: first-meeting questions, pain-discovery questions, account strategy, and source/evidence appendix.

Product bet: reports become more valuable when they preserve the session path and evidence, not just final text.

## Later Roadmap

Later expansion can include additional procurement sources, SWZ/OPZ document fetching and parsing, industry-specific taxonomies, CRM or sales workflow integrations, team-level saved analyses, alerting for new buyer/supplier/category activity, and more advanced graph analytics.

## Backlog Inputs

The roadmap is fed by:

- user feedback,
- repeated search patterns,
- abandoned workflows,
- exported report types,
- requested prompts,
- missing graph layers,
- sales questions the product cannot yet answer.

## Learning Goals

The roadmap keeps asking:

- which starting point creates more value: buyer, supplier, region, or segment,
- which graph layer leads most often to a report,
- which prompts produce saved findings,
- which report sections are reused or ignored,
- which data gaps block sales interpretation,
- which user actions signal readiness for the next workflow step.
