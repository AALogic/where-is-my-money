# Product Overview

## Product Name

**Where is my money?**

## Category

Internal sales intelligence for public procurement analysis.

## Audience

The product is built for sales teams working with Polish public-sector buyers, initially in the IT market.

The first users are account managers and salespeople preparing for public-sector conversations. Sales leaders can use the same view to understand regional or segment-level patterns.

## Problem

The data exists, but the sales answer is buried.

A tender record can tell you what was published. It does not immediately tell you whether a buyer is locked into a few suppliers, whether the same companies keep winning, whether a category is growing, or whether a supplier is a direct competitor or a possible partner.

That gap is where the product sits.

## Product Idea

The product turns procurement records into a network intelligence workflow.

It starts with structured procurement data, builds a relationship graph, lets the user explore cash-flow patterns, and then enriches the selected context with AI-assisted public research.

The end result is not simply more data. The output is a clearer sales move: who to study, what to ask, what relationship to avoid, and where a partnership angle may exist.

## Core Insight

The useful question is not only:

> Who won this tender?

The stronger question is:

> Where does this buyer's money repeatedly go, and what does that pattern mean for the next sales conversation?

## First Release Focus

The first release is buyer-centric.

The user enters a buyer NIP, sees historical procurement activity, identifies the suppliers that win most often, checks what was bought and for what value, explores the relationship graph, optionally enriches the current board with AI research, and exports a PDF report.

That path is narrow on purpose. It gives the product a clear first job before expanding into supplier-first or segment-first workflows.

## Product Positioning

This is not a generic tender search engine with AI bolted on.

It is an account intelligence tool for salespeople who need to understand public-sector buyer behavior, supplier concentration, competitive pressure, and partnership opportunities.
