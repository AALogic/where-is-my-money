# Sample Report Structure

## Report Type

Buyer sales intelligence report.

This report is generated after the user explores a buyer, graph relationships, supplier context, and optional AI research enrichment.

## Report Outline

### 1. Executive Summary

Short answer to:

- what this buyer purchases,
- who wins most often,
- how concentrated the supplier network is,
- what sales angle appears most promising.

### 2. Buyer Profile

Suggested content:

- buyer name and identifiers,
- region,
- analyzed time range,
- total notices,
- result notices,
- known spend,
- top procurement categories.

### 3. Spend And Activity Timeline

Suggested content:

- yearly spend,
- monthly or quarterly activity,
- recent tenders,
- recurring procurement cycles,
- periods with unusual activity.

### 4. Top Suppliers

Suggested content:

- suppliers ranked by wins,
- suppliers ranked by value,
- delivered products or services,
- recurring relationship patterns,
- direct competitor or potential partner interpretation.

### 5. Category And Technology Signals

Suggested content:

- CPV/category breakdown,
- detected technology or product signals,
- repeated solution areas,
- gaps or emerging demand.

### 6. Network Interpretation

Suggested content:

- graph summary,
- concentrated vs fragmented relationship map,
- dominant supplier paths,
- supplier overlaps,
- region or segment context.

### 7. AI Research Findings

Suggested content:

- selected prompt,
- research scope,
- sourced web findings,
- hypotheses,
- limitations.

### 8. Sales Strategy

Suggested content:

- account entry angle,
- competitor or partner interpretation,
- likely buyer pains,
- proposed first conversation path,
- risks to validate.

### 9. First-Meeting Questions

Suggested content:

- discovery questions,
- budget and timing questions,
- supplier relationship questions,
- pain-discovery questions,
- questions designed to reveal gaps.

### 10. Evidence Appendix

Suggested content:

- procurement data references,
- tender IDs,
- source URLs,
- AI research sources,
- generated-at timestamp,
- data limitations.

## Report Quality Bar

A report is useful when it is:

- account-specific,
- grounded in procurement history,
- clear about evidence,
- clear about hypotheses,
- actionable for a salesperson,
- readable before a meeting in less than 10 minutes.
